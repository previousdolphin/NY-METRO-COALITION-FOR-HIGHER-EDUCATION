The Manifesto

The NY Metro Coalition for Higher Ed is a group of faculty and staff unions and advocacy groups committed to advancing the right to learn, teach, research, associate, assemble, and dissent. We champion and defend the rights to academic freedom and freedom of speech as the cornerstone of higher education, crucial to the role of education in a just, democratic society. We stand against threats to ourselves and our educational institutions, which undermine or punish us for political speech, discriminate against us or harm us based on our identities or citizenship status, diminish our scientific, social, humanistic, and artistic research, or attack our rights.

We believe in the necessity for greater economic equity within and across our institutions, and uphold a vision of higher education in which the pursuit of learning does not incur crippling debt. We are an advocacy network that shares resources and support through events and other platforms. We aim to enact changes within our institutions and at local and state levels that enshrine our rights, and pressure our institutional leaders to defend the autonomy of higher education.

We aim to defend higher education from an unprecedented wave of authoritarian assault. Deadly cuts to scientific research, racist anti-DEI policies, attacks on trans people, and the abductions of pro-Palestinian student protestors do not merely target individual institutions. They threaten our whole sector.

They affect large research universities and small teaching colleges alike; four- as well as two-year schools; public as well as private institutions. They take aim not only at isolated issues, but censor teaching and threaten research across all disciplines, obstructing learning in the laboratory and the classroom. They undermine faculty governance and academic freedom for tenured, contingent, and contract faculty, as well as citizen and noncitizen colleagues and students.

To defeat this broadside against our sector requires unity and action by all who work and study on our campuses. The NY Metro area is home to one of the largest and most diverse higher education labor ecosystems in the country. We represent tens of thousands of educators and workers, and we teach hundreds of thousands of students from all over the world. The current administration has tried to bring the force of the federal government to bear on the most fundamental aspects of higher education, but they have moved quickly and recklessly. We have great strength in our numbers and our ability to act collectively. When we come together our power as workers and educators is greater than theirs.

Our communities flourish when our universities thrive. We come together to build a future for higher education free from coercion, censorship, propaganda, transphobia, racism, and anti-immigrant hate. We are taking the fight to the State House, the White House, the boardroom, and the streets. We stand up for each other— because when we fight together, we win.
